Community Support & Acknowledgements

A COMMUNAL
EFFORT

This project would not exist without the generosity of many in our community.

Shipwreck City has been shaped by the kindness of individuals and organizations across the Seattle maritime community — people who opened their docks, lent their vessels, wrote letters on our behalf, and introduced us to the right people at the right time. This page is a record of that generosity.

Marinas & Access

The following marinas have granted access to their docks and waterways, making it possible to stage equipment and access dive sites safely.

  • Boat World Marina Dock access for ROV dives on three wrecks along Westlake.

Organizations

These organizations have formally endorsed the Shipwreck City Project, made a financial donation, provided essential maritime records, or other form of support.

  • Puget Soundkeeper Alliance Letter of support recognizing the project's contribution to the documentation of Lake Union's environmental and historical record.
  • MOHAI Museum of History & Industry — letter of support acknowledging the archive's alignment with Seattle's broader maritime heritage mission.

Individuals

These friends of Shipwreck City have gone above and beyond — offering their time, vessels, knowledge, and equipment to help put robots, cameras, and sensors in the water.

  • Libby B. Historical curator, community connector, and project advisor who opened doors and elevated this project well-beyond its original means.
  • George S. Captain of a 16' Whaler, who allowed us to deploy ROVs on wrecks in central Lake Union.
  • Sean Captain of a 54' Cruiser, who allowed us to deploy ROVs on wrecks along the Ship Canal.
  • Anna B. Local marine expert who shared extensive knowledge of derelict vessels and organized several field outings.
  • Shawn A. ROV copilot and scuba expert who assisted with ROV deployments.
  • James M. ROV copilot and ocean engineer who assisted with ROV deployments.

If you would like to support Shipwreck City — whether through dock access, a letter of support, vessel time, financial donation, or a connection in the community — we would love to hear from you.

What Shipwreck City is, and why these boats matter.

The project

Shipwreck City is an ongoing effort to document, photograph, and archive the sunken vessels resting on the floor of Lake Union — one of Seattle's most historic and underexplored bodies of water. Lake Union has been a working waterway since the 1880s, and its bottom holds a remarkable record of the city's maritime past.

Most of these boats are unknown to the people who kayak and sail above them every day. Our goal is to change that — by building a public archive that treats each wreck as the historical artifact it is.

The method

Each wreck is located via side-scan sonar, then dived to collect video footage, measurements, and photographic documentation. Where possible, we cross-reference with historical records at the Washington State Archives, the Museum of History & Industry, and old newspaper archives.

All footage is shot in-water by our team of divers. We do not disturb the wrecks or remove artifacts — these are historical sites and we document them as found.

Coverage

We are currently focused on Lake Union. Future phases may expand to Lake Washington, the Ship Canal, and Puget Sound. If you have information about a wreck not in our archive, we want to hear from you.
